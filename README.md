# 🛍️ Vrinda Store — E-Commerce Sales Analysis

**An end-to-end Excel analytics project** — cleaning ~31,000 raw e-commerce orders, structuring them for analysis, and building an interactive pivot-table dashboard to uncover sales trends, customer behavior, and channel performance.

`Excel` `Power Query` `Pivot Tables` `Data Cleaning` `Dashboarding` `Data Analysis`

## 📊 About the Dataset

~31,000 individual apparel orders (kurtas, sets, tops, etc.) from an Indian online clothing store, sold across multiple e-commerce channels — **Amazon, Flipkart, Myntra, Ajio, Meesho, and Nalli** — over the course of 2022.

Fields include order ID, customer demographics (age, gender), product category & size, order status, shipping location, and order amount.

## 🔧 What I Did

| Stage | What I Did |
|---|---|
| **Data Cleaning** | Handled inconsistent formatting, missing values, and duplicate/invalid records in the raw order data |
| **Data Processing** | Structured the cleaned data for pivot-table analysis — categorized age groups, standardized state/channel names |
| **Data Analysis** | Built pivot tables to answer key business questions (see below) |
| **Dashboard** | Combined all analysis into a single interactive Excel dashboard for at-a-glance reporting |

**Business questions answered:**
- 📈 What's the monthly sales trend across 2022?
- 👥 How does revenue split by customer gender?
- 📦 What's the breakdown of order status (delivered / cancelled / returned / refunded)?
- 🗺️ Which states generate the most revenue?
- 🎂 How do sales break down by age group and gender?
- 🛒 Which sales channel performs best?

## 💡 Key Insights

- Sales **peaked in March 2022** (~₹19.3L revenue, 2,819 orders) and gradually declined toward year-end
- **Women's apparel drove ~64% of total revenue** (₹1.36 Cr) vs. ~36% from men's (₹76L)
- **Maharashtra, Karnataka, and Uttar Pradesh** were the top 3 revenue-generating states
- **Amazon (35%)** and **Myntra (23%)** were the dominant sales channels, together driving over half of all orders
- The large majority of the ~31K orders were successfully delivered, with a modest return/cancellation rate
